The PE Project is a growing team of qualified and internationally experienced Physical Educators from around the globe. Our aim is to provide friendly and accessible PE pedagogy and resources which will have an immediate impact on your lessons and students. Whatever your goal, we hope that you'll find the PE Project helpful in your journey towards excellence!

Our vision at the PE Project is pretty simple - we want to provide our PE community with an online library of pedagogically informed and concise articles on the different facets of our wonderful subject, from Assessment strategies to Teaching models. By doing so we hope that more and more PE teachers feel well-equipped with a plethora of tools and strategies which will take their lessons to the next stratisphere! We also want to keep producing high-quality PE resources which will help both specialists and non-specialists alike to lead PE in a holistic manner by emphasising the development of the whole-child (physical, social, emotional, cultural and spiritual). We would love to hear from anyone who wants to contribute to the PE Project!

At the PE Project we have currently decided upon seven-principles for ensuring high quality learning in PE:

  • We believe that students need to know that their teachers care about them. Theodore Roosevelt put it best “Nobody cares how much you know, until they know how much you care.”
  • We believe that learning should be personalised to the specific needs of each pupil, and that it is student-centered as pupils should lead their own learning and work harder than the teacher.
  • We believe that in order for pupils to be motivated learners, teachers should provide them with increasing amounts of autonomy - a sense of freedom or choice over what they are learning, and how they will learn it.
  • We believe that teachers should help students find their optimal state of flow, or Mastery so that pupils are able to find the sweet spot where the challenge is neither too easy, nor too hard.
  • We believe it is vital that pupils find purpose in their learning, and can understand how it impacts their own and others’ lives, both now and in the future.
  • we believe that all learning experience should be as authentic as possible, and should replicate closely to real-life situations, whether dealing with physical, mental or social challenges.

We have presented these principles on seven-pillars, built upon a foundation of high expectations. Please note that our principles are not set in stone, rather they are depicted as banners in front of each pillar, as we believe that as we all grow as educators so will our teaching philosophy. Therefore, we should always look for new and better ways of doing things which will ultimately shape our beliefs and philosophy.
